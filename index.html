Stephen Hawking Tribute Page

Quiet people have the loudest minds

January 8 1942 - Born in Oxford, England, Exactly 300 years after the death of Galileo.

1953 to 1958 - Attends St Albans school in North London, where he develops a passion for mathematics. 1959 to 1962 - Specialises in physics at University College Oxford. Graduates with a first class degree in natural sciences. 1963 - Begins research in cosmology and general relativity at the University of Cambridge. He is diagnosed with an ALS at the age of 21,a rare progressive disease that effects movement and speech. He is given a life expectancy of 2 years 1965 - He earns his PhD with a thesis titled 'Properties of Expanding Universes' 1965 - He marries Jane Wilde, with whom he has three children before they separate after 25 years. 1966 - Completes his doctorate and is awarded a fellowship at Gonville and Caius College, Cambridge. 1968 - He theorizes that black holes can evaporate through Hawking radiation 1970 - Discovers a remarkable property: by using quantum theory and general relativity he is able to show that black holes can emit radiation. 1973 - Joins the department of applied mathematics and theoretical physics at Cambridge. 1973 - He publishes his first academic book, 'The Large Scale Structure of Space-Time' 1974 - He becomes at 32 one of the youngest members of the Royal Society, Britain's most prestigious scientific body. 1974 - He starts using a wheelchair full-time. 1974 - He discovers that black holes could leak energy and particles into space, and even explode in a fountain of high-energy sparks. His discovery is published in the journal Nature, in a paper entitled Blackhole Explosion? 1977 - Appointed professor of gravitational physics at Cambridge. 1979 - Appointed Lucasian professor of mathematics at Cambridge (a chair held by Sir Isaac Newton in 1663). Elected as a fellow of the Royal Society. 1982 - Awarded a CBE by the Queen. 1985 - He loses the ability to speak after undergoing a tracheotomy to help him breathe after contracting pneumonia. This results eventually in his use of a computer and a voice synthesiser to communicate. 1988 - Publishes A Brief History of Time: From the Big Bang to Black Holes, a classic introduction to today's most important scientific ideas about the cosmos. Recorded in the 1998 Guinness Book of Records as an all-time bestseller. 1993 - Publishes Black Holes and Baby Universes, and other Essays, a collection of scientific articles exploring ways in which the universe may be governed. 1995 - He marries his former nurse, Elaine Mason; they too divorce after several years. 1998 - Publishes Stephen Hawking's Universe: The Cosmos Explained, a book about the basis of our existence and of everything around us. 2001 - Releases Universe in a Nutshell in the UK, a book that unravels the mysteries of recent breakthroughs in physics. 2002 - Releases On the Shoulders of Giants, The Great Works of Physics and Astronomy, an exploration of some of the greatest visionaries in the history of science including Copernicus, Kepler, Galileo, Newton and Einstein. 2002 - Publishes The Theory of Everything: The Origin and Fate of the Universe, a book that presents the most complex theories of physics past and present. 2004 - Hawking announces that he has solved the Black Hole paradox, which has been a troubling scientists for years. He presents his most recent findings at the international conference on general relativity and gravitation in Dublin. 2007 - He goes on a weightless flight in the United States as a prelude to a hoped-for sub-orbital spaceflight. 2009 - Given the highest civilian honor in the US, the Presidential Medal of Freedom 2013 - Publishes memoir, 'My Brief History' 2014 - The Hawking biopic 'The Theory of Everything', by director James Marsh, is released. British actor Eddie Redmayne goes on to win an Oscar for his portrayal of the scientist. March 14 2018 - Hawking died peacefully in his home in Cambridge, England, on the 138th anniversary of Einstein's birth. He is interred in Westminster Abbey and is remembered all across the Universe.

Sources from: The Guardian, Phys.org.
